All About Suzanne Aubert

Who was Suzanne Aubert?

Suzanne Aubert was a woman, turned priest who was born in France all the way back in 1835.
She was born in a family of 6, with her mother, her father, and 3 younger siblings.
She is mainly known for educating underdeveloped and foreign kids in Australia and New Zealand, building schools for said children with other sisters who wanted to help them as well, and was considered worthy of her being beatified many years after her untimely death in October 1st, 1926 when she was surprisingly 91 years old. Where was Suzanne Aubert born?

Suzanne Aubert was born in 1835 within a small commune somewhere around the outskirts of France called Saint-Symphorien-de-Lay.
Saint-Symphorien-de-Lay is a small commune in the countryside of France that is a part of a thing called the Loire department.
The small commune is actually only known for being the birthplace of Suzanne Aubert.
